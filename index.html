1. Abstract
Project overview & scope

This project investigates using Li-Fi (Light Fidelity) for secure communication in sensitive areas such as military bases, sea ports, and underwater.

Unlike Wi-Fi, which uses radio waves, Li-Fi uses visible or infrared light and is therefore directional, harder to intercept, and more energy efficient. Using LED transmitters, photodiodes/solar receivers, and mirror-based reflection, the system delivers fast, room-confined or short-range underwater transmissions. Proof-of-concept includes text & voice transfer using an Arduino prototype.

Quick points
  • Directional optical link → natural containment
  • Low cost Arduino prototype
  • Works in RF-sensitive areas
🚨
2. Problem Statement
Why we need Li-Fi

Near defense bases, banks, and hospitals, communication that cannot be easily intercepted is required. Submarine communication still depends on wires or expensive acoustic systems — radios fail underwater. Wi-Fi leaks beyond walls and is vulnerable to hacking.

  • Subaquatic radio is impractical — current solutions are costly or restricted.
  • RF leakage and interference pose security risks.
  • Key question: Can light + mirrors provide a secure, low-cost data channel?
Impact areas
  • Military / defense rooms
  • Hospitals (RF-sensitive zones)
  • Underwater sensor links

5. Conceptual Design
Transmitter, receiver & underwater setup

5.1 Transmitter

  • Arduino encodes text/audio into framed packets.
  • LED is modulated at high speed (beyond human perception).
  • Mirrors deflect beams for non-line-of-sight routing.

5.2 Receiver

  • Photodiode detects fluctuations; TIA amplifier cleans the signal.
  • Arduino decodes packets back to text/audio.

5.3 Underwater Setup

  • Use IP68 enclosures, anti-reflective windows and mirrors for underwater paths.

6. Applications
Where Li-Fi can be useful
  • Military & Airports: Secure in-room links, no RF leakage.
  • Hospitals: Communications that don't disturb equipment.
  • Underwater research: Send commands to ROVs & sensors.
  • Banks: Confidential in-room data transfer.
  • Smart cities: Li-Fi lampposts as localized nodes.

7. Benefits
Why Li-Fi is advantageous
  • Potentially faster optical channels than some RF links.
  • Light cannot pass through walls — natural security.
  • Works in RF-sensitive environments.
  • Energy efficient — LEDs provide light + data.
⚠️
8. Limitations
Constraints to consider
  • Requires line-of-sight or mirror reflections; alignment matters.
  • Outdoor sunlight may create noise and reduce reliability.
  • Waterproof housings raise cost and require careful build quality.
🧪
9. Demonstration Plan
Step-by-step testing (classroom demo)
  1. Assemble TX: LED + MOSFET driver + Arduino. Use safe current limits and heatsink.
  2. Place photodiode receiver 1–2 m away; calibrate threshold (amplifier/comparator).
  3. Send text via Serial Monitor; verify receiver prints characters.
  4. Use mirror to redirect beam around an obstacle and verify reception.
  5. Seal modules in clear housings and repeat test in a shallow tub or bucket.

10. Future Work
How to improve & scale
  • Infrared LEDs for invisible indoor comms and better SNR.

  • Implement Manchester encoding, CRC, and adaptive thresholds.

12. Safety Notes
Precautions for experiments
  • Do not use high-power lasers without proper training & eye protection.
  • Keep LED currents within datasheet limits; use drivers/heatsinks where needed.
  • Test waterproofing at low voltage only (≤ 5 V) before deeper submergence.
